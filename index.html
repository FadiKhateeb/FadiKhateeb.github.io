Fadi Khatib // Under Reconstruction

I am a master student in the Department of Computer Science and Applied Mathematics at the Weizmann Institute of Science under the supervision of Prof. Ronen Basri.

I received my B.Sc in Computer Science at the age of 18 from the University of Haifa through the Challenge program, which is designed for exceptional high school students.

My research interests lie at the intersection of computer vision, computer graphics, and machine learning. Through my research, I want to make it easier for computers and machines to understand and capture the dynamic 3D world we live in from images and videos.

Research
GRelPose: Generalizable End-to-End Relative Camera Pose Regression
Fadi Khatib*, Yuval Margalit*, Meirav Galun, Ronen Basri
Preprint, 2022
/ ArXiv / Project Page /

We proposes a generalizable, end-to-end deep learning-based method for relative pose regression between two images, by utilizing coarse features from the pre-trained local feature matching architecture - LoFTR. Our method works exceptionally well with limited training data.
